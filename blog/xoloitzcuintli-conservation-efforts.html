Mexico’s Spiritual Guide

With more than 3,500 years of history, the Xoloitzcuintle is more than a dog; it is a living symbol of Mexican culture. Its story is woven with gods, artists, and the struggle for cultural survival. Explore the milestones that have defined this unique breed.

📜

Aztec Mythology

In the Aztec worldview, the Xolo was created by the god Xólotl to protect the living and guide the souls of the dead through the perilous underworld, Mictlán—a sacred companion and spiritual guardian.

🎨

Art & Culture

Immortalized by masters like Diego Rivera and Frida Kahlo, the Xolo became an icon of post-revolutionary Mexican nationalism—representing Indigenous heritage and pride in the nation’s pre-Hispanic roots.

💔

Near Extinction

After the Spanish conquest, the breed was pushed to the brink of extinction. Seen as “pagan” and even consumed as food, Xolos survived for centuries only in remote rural areas of Mexico, preserving their ancient lineage in secret.

The Rebirth of a Breed

The Xoloitzcuintle’s story is not one of permanent endangerment but of extraordinary recovery. Thanks to dedicated efforts in the 20th century, the breed was saved from oblivion. The chart below illustrates this successful comeback—proof of Mexico’s commitment to its living heritage.
